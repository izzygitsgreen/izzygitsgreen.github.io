Experience.io - find and book experiences and travel, all in the same place

(a concept project)

I get inspired by different places and destinations on Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest all the time that I want to go to, but there is no simple way to reserve and book from the apps. I created Experience.io as a place to do both.

Problem: Users are able to find experiences and destinations they love on a variety of popular social media apps, but the reservation and booking capabilities of traditional travel sites are not present, creating a lot of extra work for users to reserve and book the experiences they find.


The Project: Create a platform that allows users to find experiences and destinations they love, while also allowing them the reservation and booking capabilities of a traditional travel site.


Type: A concept project

Methods: Brainstorming, User Personas, User Survey, Market Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Visual Design, Iteration


Tools: Sketching, Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Miro, Canva, Fontawesome, Unsplash


Duration: 2 weeks


Languages: HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript

Context:

Booking flights with American Airlines is tedious due to the amount of options available and the distracting content on the landing page that clutters the booking area with advertisements and unnecessary popups.

I created a more streamlined booking process for American Airlines travelers that minimizes clutter and highlights features that promote booking travel easily.


Research

User Survey:

I conducted research by using a survey sent out to users in my target group to fill out. I then went through the responses and used the relevant information to inform my decisions about the product

Which services do you use to book experiences and trips? Check all that apply.




Where do you go for travel inspiration? Check all that apply.




Question: Would you book your experiences and travel from an app such as Instagram or TikTok if you could? If yes, please explain briefly. If no, please explain briefly.

  • 54% of users would book travel and excursions from a social media app
  • 46% of users would not book travel and excursions from a social media app



Question: Infinite scroll is the endless feed of content that you can scroll through when using apps like Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, Pinterest, etc. Do you like the concept of infinite scroll when using your social media apps? If yes, please explain briefly. If no, please explain briefly.

  • 73% of users enjoy infinite scroll UI
  • 27% of users do not enjoy infinite scroll UI

Key Takeaway

  • Majority of participants are comfortable booking travel on a variety of different services as long as those services are secure (i.e. exclusively for travel, careful with credit card information and personal data)




Key Takeaway

  • Users reported that they use social media for travel and experience inspiration, but go to travel sites for booking - if the inspiration and booking site could be one then this would enable both inspiration and booking to occur on the same platform





Key Takeaways

  • An overwhelming majority of participants get travel inspiration from social media apps
  • 54% of users would book travel and excursions from a social media app if the process was user friendly and included realistic pricing/date options and security features for their data
  • 46% of users would not book travel on a social media app because of the lack of data security they feel social media apps contain

Key Takeaways

  • 73% of users enjoyed infinite scroll UI
  • Users both definitively for and against infinite scroll in apps said that it allows for the most satisfying user experience, but feeds their app addiction and wastes time
  • 18% of participants went as far to say that an app without infinite scroll would seem like it had worse UI than one that did

Summary of Wireframe Iteration:


  • Login/signup box was removed from the center of the page and is now located in the top right hand corner
  • Scrolling advertisements were removed and repositioned in the second section of the landing page
  • Removed the "redeem miles" option
  • Combined the "round trip" and "one way" options into an interactive toggle
  • Consolidated the top navigation map to the three most important areas users need
  • Only left the "Flights" and "Hotels" buttons in the booking area

Iteration:



Due to time constraints, I was unable to conduct a large set of user tests. However, I was able to iterate on this prototype using the feedback gathered.


Several users went through the final high fidelity prototype and provided feedback



Key Takeaways


Things to Keep

  1. The cleaner layout
  2. The destinations within driving distance (very timely due to COVID-19)
  3. The increased visibility of key pieces of information they look for (i.e. bookings, rescheduling flights, refunds, discounts, etc.)
  4. The increased interactive element on the page dedicated to COVID-19 information

Things to Reconsider

  1. Content on using points should be front and center
  2. The content looked too big on the screen, and more background space was required
  3. There needed to be a button for "flights" in the booking section like there was for "hotels" and "cars"

Final Product

Changes Made


  • Brightened up the website background to make it more inviting to travelers
  • Simplified the booking experience to one bar in the middle of the page
  • Reduced the amount of inputs users needed to enter when booking travel
  • Provided the option to travel to a specific destination, along with the option for users to explore destinations

Retrospective:


Changes I would make in the future to get more accurate results...

  • Increase the sample size of users interviewed
  • Examine data from American Airlines to determine which features on the site need to be accentuated because they have business impact
  • Get feedback from my final prototypes from a greater number of users
  • Get data from AA on how users most frequently sign up for their loyalty program to see if this can take up reduced space on the landing page while still attracting new users
