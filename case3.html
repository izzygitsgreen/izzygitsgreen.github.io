KFresh Market

(a client project)

The Kinnelon Farmer's Market, "KFresh Market", is launching this summer, and I worked with the organization to improve it's vendor registration process through the research, ideation, wireframing and prototyping, and iteration phases to improve the experience.

Final Product

Incorporating research and wireframes

Problem: Vendors were finding it difficult to register for the market due to the confusing process of having to submit several PDF documents, as well as register online with the town's recreation department.

The Project: Study and improve the KFresh Market registration process for vendors so that they can register easily, and do not need to reach out to the market team for assistance.

Methods: Brainstorming, Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Visual Design, User Testing, Iteration

Tools: Sketching, Miro, Adobe Illustrator, Unsplash, Network Solutions CMS

Duration: 1 week

Type: Client Project

Design Process

Empathize



User Base

  • Vendors
  • Market team

Demographic

  • Vendors: Local farmers and craftsmen in the Northern New Jersey area
  • Market Team: Community members, small businesses, and volunteers working to bring the market together

Pain Points

  • The market team wanted as many vendors as possible to register for the market, but the registration process was slowing down their pace
  • The registration process was not clearly documented on the website, and required vendors to download and print PDF forms, as well as create an online account with the town recreation department
  • The market team was spending many hours fielding questions from vendors on how to register, which was taking time away from market operations



Define


Problem Statement

Vendors were finding it difficult to register for the market due to the undocumented process of having to submit several PDF documents, as well as register online with the town's recreation department.

Testing & Market Research

  • I went through the existing registration process as a test user to better understand the steps
  • The process seemed to be lacking a broad overview of what was needed, and clear steps for users to follow
  • I also researched the registration processes of several other local farmers markets to review their processes.


  • The "West Milford Farmer's Market" included an online form for potential vendors to submit their interest through
  • The "Hoboken Farmers' Market" included a contact form for any vendors who were interested in selling at the market to fill out
  • The "Montclair Farmers' Market" directed potential vendors to their contact form to reach out about registering as a vendor.

Test


I tested the registration process with colleagues, the market team, and select vendors.


Key changes made from the original registration process, to the revised.


  • Highlighted the two step process to register as a vendor at the top of the page so it captures attention
  • Created a separate section for Required Forms and Community Pass so that the process was split into parts
  • Under the "Required Forms" area, I expanded the box containing the "checklist" so that users would click that first when beginning the form process.

Results of Testing


  • Breaking the process into parts worked well to improve user understanding and ease at moving through the process
  • The process of downloading and submitting PDF forms is still a challenging user experience
  • Breaking the steps down for different user scenarios worked very well, and this was incorporated into the live site as well

Retrospective:


Changes I would make given more time and resources...

  • Test the site with market attendees instead of only with colleagues and the market team
  • Include images from the market to allow the site to better connect with community members
  • Digitized the forms so that users could fill them out online and save the hassle of downloading, printing and submitting them. We explores this, but it cost too many resources at this point to involve a third party service to validate the signatures on the digital forms.
