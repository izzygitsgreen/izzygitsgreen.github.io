Redesign of the American Airlines landing page to optimize booking travel - a concept project


I spent 2020 rearranging travel itineraries with American Airlines, so I redesigned the experience.

Problem: How can I make it easier and faster for users to book travel on the American Airlines website?


The Project: As a frequent flyer of American Airlines and avid user of their website, I redesigned the web experience for better usability and cleaner design with the goal of allowing users to book travel more quickly and with greater ease.


Type: A concept project

Methods: User Personas, Comparative Analysis, Wireframing, Prototyping, Visual Design


Tools: Figma, Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Miro, Canva, Fontawesome,


Duration: 3 weeks


Languages: HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript

Context:

Booking flights with American Airlines is tedious due to the amount of options available and the distracting content on the landing page that clutters the booking area with advertisements and unnecessary popups.

I created a more streamlined booking process for American Airlines travelers that minimizes clutter and highlights features that promote booking travel easily.


Research

Competitive Analysis:

I reviewed 3 different competitors to American Airlines to see how they structure their landing pages differently.










Key Takeaways: Delta

  • The landing page is busy, and includes information on a variety of topics
  • The booking area is cleaner and more straightforward for users, and is also located at the top so it is the first thing they see
  • Lots of space is used for rewards programs
  • An image of confused travelers is displayed

Key Takeaways: American

  • The landing page is busy due to flashing images, but includes a good amount of white space
  • The booking area has a lot of information present within it
  • The flashing images are often of enticing destinations
  • The rewards program is mentioned 3 times on the top part of the landing page

Key Takeaways: Airbnb

  • The landing page is clean, and the booking area sits at the top so it is the first thing users see
  • There are fewer tabs in the navigation bar compared to the Delta site
  • Attractive background image that inspires travel
  • Navigation bar only features a few options, making the overall landing page appear less cluttered

Key Takeaways: Expedia

  • Comparitively clean landing page
  • Many tabs in the booking area, but not an overabundance of navigation bar tabs
  • Contains an attractive background image
  • COVID-19 information is easily accessible immediately

User Personas

I interviewed 10 people across the United States, and will highlight my findings from the 2 interviewees who fit the target audience I designed for through my user personas.

Key Takeaways

  • Users wanted more interactive components on the site
  • Users wanted less unnecessary components to have to sift through when booking travel
  • Users wanted more emphasis on the areas they cared about (i.e. itinerary change, reimbursements, and cancellations)

Summary of Wireframe Iteration:


  • Login/signup box was removed from the center of the page and is now located in the top right hand corner
  • Scrolling advertisements were removed and repositioned in the second section of the landing page
  • Removed the "redeem miles" option
  • Combined the "round trip" and "one way" options into an interactive toggle
  • Consolidated the top navigation map to the three most important areas users need
  • Only left the "Flights" and "Hotels" buttons in the booking area

Iteration:



Due to time constraints, I was unable to conduct a large set of user tests. However, I was able to iterate on this prototype using the feedback gathered.


Several users went through the final high fidelity prototype and provided feedback



Key Takeaways


Things to Keep

  1. The cleaner layout
  2. The destinations within driving distance (very timely due to COVID-19)
  3. The increased visibility of key pieces of information they look for (i.e. bookings, rescheduling flights, refunds, discounts, etc.)
  4. The increased interactive element on the page dedicated to COVID-19 information

Things to Reconsider

  1. Content on using points should be front and center
  2. The content looked too big on the screen, and more background space was required
  3. There needed to be a button for "flights" in the booking section like there was for "hotels" and "cars"

Final Product

Changes Made


  • Brightened up the website background to make it more inviting to travelers
  • Simplified the booking experience to one bar in the middle of the page
  • Reduced the amount of inputs users needed to enter when booking travel
  • Provided the option to travel to a specific destination, along with the option for users to explore destinations

Retrospective:


Changes I would make in the future to get more accurate results...

  • Increase the sample size of users interviewed
  • Examine data from American Airlines to determine which features on the site need to be accentuated because they have business impact
  • Get feedback from my final prototypes from a greater number of users
  • Get data from AA on how users most frequently sign up for their loyalty program to see if this can take up reduced space on the landing page while still attracting new users
