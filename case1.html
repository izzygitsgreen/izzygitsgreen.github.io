Title: Redesign of American Airlines landing page to optimize booking travel - a concept project


"I spent 2020 rearranging travel itineraries with American Airlines, so I redesigned the experience."

Problem: How might we make it easier for customers to book travel on the American Airlines website?


The Project: As a frequent flyer of American Airlines and avid user of their website, I redesigned the web experience for better usability and cleaner design with the goal of allowing users to book travel more quickly and with greater ease.


Duration: 2 weeks

Methods: User Personas, Competitive/Comparative Analysis, Wireframing, Prototyping, Visual Design


Tools: Adobe XD, Miro, Canva

Before & After

Context: Booking flights with American Airlines is tedious due to the amount of options available and the distracting content on the landing page that clutters the booking area with advertisements and unecessary popups.

I worked to create a more streamlined booking process for American Airlines travelers that minimized clutter and highlighted features that promoted booking travel easily.

Research
Competitive Analysis:

  • To begin, I compared the American Airlines landing page with that of it's competitor, Delta Airlines. I also reviewed Airbnb's website because although it exists in a different vertical of the travel industry, it is known for it's clean design and ease of use
  • Market Research - include company logos here
Target Audience/Persona
  • Current users of the American Airlines website, as well as new users
  • Have to ensure this design is easy to understand for new users wanting to book travel
  • There cannot be a disconnect between the current website and the website I will be designing since users are accustomed to the current site

Meet the User

Goals:

  • He wishes booking was less tedious online

  • He wishes American Airlines had a map feature where he can choose airlines based on price like Google Flights does (some competitors also have this)
Key Insights:
(icon) The most important components of flight booking to users are the price and the date/time factors. These should be made clear on the landing page. (icon) Users would like more flexible ways to explore dates and times of a variety of locations on this website. (icon) Users do not like distracting advertisements, especially when they are for very limited dates/times, or have other strings attached that are not practical for users booking travel. (icon) Most users book flights separately from hotels, so the focus here should be on the flight booking process.
Define Problem Statement
The American Airlines landing page could benefit from better usability and cleaner design, in addition to less advertisements flashing at users during the booking process. It also lacks an efficient way to explore additional locations and dates/times for travel, and diverts the user's attention away from the primary focus of booking flights due to the amount of information and other calls to action on the landing page.

Key Changes:
  • The AA login/signup box was removed from the center of the page and is still located in the top right hand corner for users to sign up with
  • The scrolling advertisements were moved lower into the "Today's Offers" section of the site to keep them all together and to minimize user distraction while booking
  • Removed the "redeem miles" option because this should appear in the checkout process where users can choose to purchase with cash/credit or redeem their miles
  • I combined the "round trip" and "one way" options into an interactive toggle to save space and make it stand out more
  • Consolidated the top navigation map to the three most important areas users would need to search for and moved the previous information elsewhere
Iteration:
  • After going through the wireframes and having users test them, I realized they were a cleaner version of the current AA landing page
  • I wanted to create a transformed experience while still keeping booking travel with AA a top priority for users

Final Wireframe & Prototype

-Show the final product embedded in the page (inside a computer desktop frame), and then highlight the features you changed one by one Changes Made

Retrospective:
Changes I would make in the future to get more accurate results...
  • Increase the sample size of users interviewed
  • Examine data from American Airlines to determine which features on the site need to be accentuated because they have business impact
  • Get feedback from my final prototypes from a greater number of users
  • Get data from AA on how users most frequently sign up for their loyalty program to see if this can take up reduced space on the landing page while still attracting new users
